About

Biography

My research sits at the intersection of computational science and public policy. Starting from a PhD in Physics, I now study India's administrative evolution, education governance, and public health — bringing quantitative rigour to historical and policy questions. I lead IndiaStateStory, tracing India's district boundaries from 1872 to the present.

Mapping the Republic: Visual Story of India's States and Districts, 1950–2025

Publisher: Astitva Prakashan | Published: 20 March 2026 | Format: Hardcover

This book is both a historical atlas and visual archive of how states and districts formed and evolved in modern India. It addresses the lack of comprehensive tracing of India's evolving political and administrative boundaries post-independence, providing a visual narrative of how India's states and districts came to be and how they changed over time.

It chronicles the transformation of India's internal map — from British provinces and Princely states in 1947 to the states, Union territories and districts of today. Using decade-wise maps, timelines, alluvial charts and images, it traces the political and administrative boundary changes in each state from 1950 to 2025. The book draws from and acts as a companion piece to the India State Story project.

Designed for students, teachers, researchers, public administrators, UPSC aspirants, and general readers seeking foundational context for India's federal and territorial structure.
